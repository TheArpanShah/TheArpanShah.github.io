How to Lock Low Energy Prices in Illinois

by Energy Bill Quote

Posted on January 14th, 2015


Oil and gasoline prices are at a 6 year low. In some areas, it’s even hit as low as $1.99! But did you know that electricity and natural gas rates have also hit multi-year lows? Check out the graph below from the Chicago Mercantile Exchange:



Here is the graph for natural gas, an even better looking chart:



What if you could lock in $2.00 gasoline prices for years to come? Wouldn’t everyone love that. However, as a business, you HAVE that advantage to lock in rates for electricity and natural gas. By dealing directly with an energy supplier, you may be able to lock in favorable terms for 1, 2, and even 3 years. Even if the market moves up (and it will up at some point), you will still pay a lower rate. A significant number of Illinois small businesses have switched to 3rd party supplier. But now is a great time to get a quote and see if you’re overpaying or not.

Here’s how it works:

ComEd still delivers your bill and electricity, the only difference is a 3rd party buys your energy on the open market and partners with ComEd to supply it. Currently, this process is called energy deregulation and is supported by Illinois State Law.
